## Problem Statement

Supervising physicians (preceptors) are required to complete trainee assessment forms (T-Res, One45) after clinical encounters, but they consistently delay, under-fill, or skip them entirely. Preceptors earn $200+/hr and won't spend 5-10 minutes on a form that delivers zero value back to them. They ignore email reminders and don't remember logins.

The result: residents wait weeks to months for feedback, often fill out their own evaluations and check an "endorsed" box (sometimes falsely), and training programs have poor-quality, incomplete data for competency tracking and accreditation.

The irony: **rich verbal feedback already happens** — preceptors routinely give detailed spoken feedback after patient encounters. The problem isn't that feedback doesn't exist. It's that no one captures it, and the form is a separate, disconnected task.

## Status Quo

The current workflow for a preceptor-trainee assessment:

1. Resident/medical student joins a preceptor for a day or week. Sometimes the preceptor doesn't know they're coming.
2. Trainee sees patients, does histories/physicals, presents plans. Preceptor provides verbal feedback after encounters and at end of day.
3. Preceptor is supposed to log into T-Res or One45 and fill out an assessment form.
4. Preceptor ignores email reminders. Doesn't remember login. Doesn't want to spend 5-10 minutes typing.
5. Rotation leads batch-fill forms at end of rotation from fading memory, producing brief, generic assessments.
6. Only 1 of 9 rotations amalgamates daily preceptor feedback (via Qualtrics survey).
7. Residents frequently fill out their own forms and mark them as "endorsed" — or just never get assessed.

**The status quo is the real competitor.** The verbal feedback happens and vanishes. The form either gets filled badly or doesn't get filled at all. "Nothing" is the current solution for most encounters.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary user:** The resident (initiates the recording, reviews the output, exports/submits the form). The preceptor is the data source, not the user — they just talk.

**Target preceptor archetype:** Dr. George Chang — general surgeon, runs a 2-week Family Medicine rotation, coordinates 1 resident + 1 medical student, full patient slate, provides good verbal feedback but fills out the form 1-2 months later (if at all). Already uses an AI scribe for patient notes. Would adopt this if: (a) the resident initiates it, (b) he does zero setup/login/follow-up, (c) he can "word vomit and never think about it again."

**Resident incentive:** Residents are type-A personalities who want their record to reflect their actual performance. It is morally distressing to receive rich verbal feedback and then see a generic, inadequate written form in their program file. Debrief gives residents a way to capture proof of their own competence. They're not reluctant users — they're motivated self-advocates.

**Narrowest wedge:** UBC Family Medicine, one program, one PD who's said yes. Multi-form template engine built from day one (boil the lake — form generation is cheap with LLMs).

## Constraints

- Must be resident-initiated — zero preceptor onboarding or login required
- Must work in clinical settings — hallways, offices, between patients
- Audio quality varies — background noise, quick conversations
- Privacy/consent: must handle recording consent explicitly before any audio capture
- Must map to existing competency frameworks (CanMEDS roles for Canadian programs)
- Must produce output that satisfies accreditation requirements
- Institutional ethics/privacy review likely required for pilot at UBC
- No patient data should be captured — feedback is about the trainee's performance, not patient information

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "Voice Memo → Form" (Minimal Viable)
Mobile-first web app. Resident taps record, preceptor speaks feedback, audio goes to STT → LLM extracts structured assessment → populates form template. Resident reviews and submits. No backend platform — just capture, structure, export PDF.

- **Effort:** S (human: ~2 weeks / CC: ~2-3 hours)
- **Risk:** Low
- **Pros:** Ships fastest, validates core hypothesis
- **Cons:** No longitudinal tracking, no program-level views

### Approach B: "Assessment Platform" (Ideal Architecture)
Full platform replacing T-Res/One45. Voice capture + LLM structuring + longitudinal trainee profiles + PD dashboard + analytics. Multi-form support from day one.

- **Effort:** M (human: ~2 months / CC: ~1-2 weeks)
- **Risk:** Medium
- **Pros:** The actual product the PD wants to buy
- **Cons:** More to build before first user touches it

### Approach C: "Trojan Horse" (Phone Number)
No app. A Twilio phone number the resident calls. Preceptor speaks into the phone. Structured assessment emailed back as PDF within minutes. Zero install, zero login.

- **Effort:** S (human: ~1 week / CC: ~1-2 hours)
- **Risk:** Low-Med
- **Pros:** Absolute minimum friction, great for old-school physicians who like dictation
- **Cons:** No tracking, harder consent flow, audio quality risk

## Recommended Approach

**Hybrid: Platform architecture with MVP features + phone intake channel.**

Build on platform bones (Approach B) — proper data model, auth, multi-form template engine — so there's no rewrite later. But launch the pilot with Approach A's simplicity: voice capture → LLM structuring → PDF export. Layer in Approach C's phone number as an alternative intake channel for preceptors who prefer dictation-style.

**What ships for the pilot:**
- Mobile-first web app (PWA — no app store needed)
- Resident creates a session: selects preceptor, rotation, form type
- One-tap recording with consent confirmation
- Audio → speech-to-text (Whisper/Deepgram) → LLM extraction (Claude) → structured assessment
- Resident reviews transcript + auto-generated form fields + CanMEDS competency mapping
- Export as PDF formatted to match the required institutional form
- Twilio phone channel: **deferred to post-pilot** (consent flow complexity not worth solving before core web flow is validated)
- Auto-email feedback summary to preceptor after export (trust-building, closes the loop)
- Lightweight pilot metrics page (total sessions, completion rate, unique preceptors/residents)
- Audio playback in review screen (resident can replay recording alongside transcript)
- One45-compatible CSV export (contingent on getting import format from PD in Week 1)

**What comes later (post-pilot, as data accumulates):**
- PD dashboard with completion rates and aggregate analytics
- Longitudinal trainee profiles showing competency progression
- Institutional export/integration with One45 or T-Res
- Multi-program support (surgery, psychiatry, internal medicine, etc.)
- Analytics showing growth over time — actionable for both positive and negative feedback

Audio files are stored encrypted at rest (AES-256) in Canadian cloud storage. Audio is retained for 90 days post-transcription (configurable by program), then auto-deleted. Transcripts and assessments are retained per institutional policy.

## Authentication & Authorization

- **Residents:** Email magic link (no password to forget). Scoped to their program. Can only see their own sessions and assessments.
- **Preceptors:** No account. No login. They are identified by the resident selecting them from a program-provided roster (dropdown, not free-text). Preceptor records are seeded from program data.
- **Program Directors (post-pilot):** SSO via institutional IdP (UBC CWL / Shibboleth) when the dashboard ships. For pilot, PD receives exported PDFs.

## PHI/PII Protection

Patient data leakage is a regulatory requirement, not an open question. The pipeline handles it:

1. **Regex/NER pass (first):** Immediately after speech-to-text, a fast regex/NER pass catches obvious PHI patterns (MRN formats like `C###-####`, phone numbers, dates of birth). Cheap and fast — runs before transcript hits the LLM.
2. **LLM scrubbing pass (second):** A dedicated LLM call scans the partially-redacted transcript for contextual PHI that regex can't catch (patient names mentioned in conversation, implicit identifiers). Remaining PHI is redacted (replaced with `[PATIENT]`, `[MRN]`, etc.) before storage or assessment extraction.
3. **Prompt engineering:** The assessment extraction prompt explicitly instructs the LLM to focus on trainee performance only and to exclude any patient-identifying information from structured output.
4. **Retroactive redaction:** If PHI is detected post-storage (false negative discovered later), the system supports retroactive redaction with an audit trail.
4. **Audit log:** All redactions are logged (count and type, not content) for compliance reporting.

## Error Handling & Edge Cases

| Scenario | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| Vague feedback ("they did fine") | LLM marks affected fields as "Insufficient detail — manual input needed." Resident prompted to fill gaps or re-record. |
| Unintelligible audio | STT confidence score below threshold → resident sees warning: "Audio quality too low. Try re-recording in a quieter setting." Session saved as draft. |
| LLM extraction confidence low | Each structured field has a confidence indicator (high/medium/low). Fields below medium are highlighted yellow for resident review. |
| Offline / poor connectivity | Audio recorded locally on device (PWA with MediaRecorder API). Upload queued and retried when connectivity returns. Resident sees "Saved locally — will process when online." |
| LLM hallucination | Resident is the quality gate — they review every field before export. Transcript is shown side-by-side with extracted fields so discrepancies are visible. |

## Technical Risks

### Risk 1: LLM Extraction Accuracy (HIGH)
Mapping unstructured verbal feedback to structured CanMEDS competency ratings is the core technical bet.

**Mitigation:**
- **Prompt engineering spike (Week 1):** Collect 5-10 sample transcripts (synthetic or from consenting preceptors). Test extraction against the actual UBC FM T-Res form template. Have the PD or a rotation lead rate the LLM output vs. a manually-filled form.
- **Accuracy target:** Competency mapping matches expert rater ≥80% of the time on a 5-point scale (within 1 level).
- **Evaluation method:** Blind comparison — PD rates 10 LLM-generated assessments and 10 manually-filled assessments without knowing which is which.
- **Fallback:** If accuracy is insufficient, the LLM pre-fills fields as suggestions and the resident completes/corrects. This is still faster than the current workflow.

### Risk 2: Data Residency & Compliance
Canadian data residency (PIPEDA) + audio storage requires:
- **Hosting:** Supabase ca-central-1 (all-in-one: DB, Storage, Auth, Edge Functions).
- **Encryption:** AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit.
- **Audio retention:** 90 days post-transcription, then auto-deleted. Configurable per program.
- **Access logging:** All data access logged for audit trail.
- **STT provider:** Deepgram (SaaS API, zero-ops, Canadian data processing available). Evaluate self-hosted Whisper if costs exceed $500/month or if institutional policy requires on-premise processing.

### Risk 3: Phone Channel Consent
The Twilio phone channel is **deferred to post-pilot.** The consent flow for a phone call (no visual UI for confirmation) adds complexity that isn't worth solving before the core web flow is validated. The web PWA is the pilot intake channel. Phone channel ships after pilot proves demand.

## Form Template Engine

Form templates are JSON schemas defining field names, field types (rating scale, free text, checkbox), competency mappings (CanMEDS roles), and rating scale labels. For the pilot, templates are manually authored by the developer from the actual UBC FM T-Res and One45 forms. The LLM extraction prompt is parameterized by the template — it receives the field definitions and outputs structured JSON matching that schema. Post-pilot, a PD-facing template editor is planned so new programs can define their own forms without developer involvement.

### Extraction Modes

The template engine supports two extraction modes depending on the form type:

**Mode 1: Multi-output (T-Res Field Notes)**
One conversation generates MULTIPLE field notes (1-5), each capturing a distinct observed activity. The LLM detects topic shifts and distinct patient encounters in the transcript and generates separate field notes for each. Signals for splitting: explicit patient transitions ("and then with your next patient..."), distinct skill areas discussed, and transcript length (longer debriefs → more field notes). Maximum 5 field notes per conversation.

Each field note contains:
- Activity type (Field Note)
- Coaching: "Something you did well..." / "Consider next time you might..."
- Tags: Skill Dimension (CanMEDS), Priority Topics, Domain of Care
- Preceptor endorsement

**Mode 2: Single-output (One45 Daily Evaluation)**
One conversation generates ONE holistic evaluation form. All feedback is synthesized into a single assessment with ratings across CanMEDS sub-skills (or role-level binary ratings), plus narrative strengths/needs improvement.

### Form Types (Pilot)

| Form | Platform | Mode | Rating Scale | Fields |
|------|----------|------|-------------|--------|
| Field Note | T-Res | Multi (1-5) | N/A (narrative + tags) | Coaching notes, Skill Dimension, Priority Topics, Domain of Care |
| Daily Evaluation | One45 (EM) | Single | 5-point Likert (Rarely Meets → Consistently Exceeds) | CanMEDS sub-skills + Strengths/Needs Improvement |
| Mid-Rotation ITAR | One45 | NOT a direct target — Debrief feeds data to rotation leads who fill this | Binary (Concerns/On target) per CanMEDS role + comments | Filled by rotation leads, not residents |

### Competency Framework (UBC Family Medicine)

Extracted from UBC FM Domains of Care document. 8 domains with core activities:
- Family Medicine Fundamentals
- Health Equity and Care of Community
- Maternity and Newborn Care
- Care of Children and Adolescents
- Care of Adults
- Care of the Elderly
- Palliative and End of Life Care
- Mental Health and Addictions Care
- Surgical and Procedural Skills

These map to T-Res "Domain of Care" tags. The LLM extraction prompt uses this vocabulary as the controlled list for tagging.

## LLM Confidence Scoring

Each extracted assessment field includes a confidence score (0.0–1.0) from the LLM via structured output (Claude's tool_use with explicit confidence fields). Calibration thresholds:
- **High (≥0.8):** Field displayed normally.
- **Medium (0.5–0.8):** Field highlighted yellow — resident should verify.
- **Low (<0.5):** Field marked "Insufficient detail — manual input needed."

Thresholds are calibrated against the evaluation set from the Week 1 prompt engineering spike and may be adjusted based on pilot data.

## Distribution Plan

- Green dot: "Ready"
- Gray dot: "Exported"

### Audio Player (Review Screen)
Sticky bottom bar, fixed at bottom of review screen. Play/pause button, scrub bar, timestamp. Always accessible while scrolling through transcript and structured fields. Like a podcast player.

### Toast Notifications
Bottom-center, dark background (#1a1a1a), white text, rounded corners (8px). 5-second auto-dismiss. "View" action button on right side. Used for: processing complete, export success, upload status.

### User Journey Emotional Arc
1. First open → skeptical → empty state with clear first action eases anxiety
2. Create session → slightly anxious → simple dropdown form (not free text)

7. Export PDF → accomplishment → "My record reflects reality"
8. Repeat user: progress count after export ("4 assessments captured this rotation")
